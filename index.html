⚠️ Government Health Warning Statement — 27 CFR Part 16 (mandatory on ALL alcohol beverages)

Required verbatim on every alcohol beverage containing ≥ 0.5% ABV, domestic and imported, per the Alcoholic Beverage Labeling Act of 1988.

GOVERNMENT WARNING: (1) According to the Surgeon General, women should not drink alcoholic beverages during pregnancy because of the risk of birth defects. (2) Consumption of alcoholic beverages impairs your ability to drive a car or operate machinery, and may cause health problems.
Formatting rules (27 CFR 16.22)
"GOVERNMENT WARNING:" must be ALL CAPS + bold
Remainder must not be bold
Must appear separate and apart from all other information
Must be on a contrasting background
Must appear as a continuous statement — cannot be split
Minimum type size
1 mm — containers ≤ 237 ml (8 fl. oz.)
2 mm — containers 237 ml – 3 L (8–101 fl. oz.)
3 mm — containers > 3 L (101 fl. oz.)
Common failure modes
"Government Warning:" (title case)
Bold on full statement (not just heading)
Wrong, paraphrased, or truncated text
Buried in other text (not separate & apart)
Type too small to be legible
Placement
May appear on any front, back, or side label
Applies to all three beverage types
